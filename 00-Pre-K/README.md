
# Pre-K Curriculum Guide

This folder is intended to support the creation of a thoughtful Pre-K curriculum that blends strengths from both the Massachusetts and New Jersey early learning approaches. The goal is to create a practical, developmentally appropriate learning path that balances strong foundational skills with interactive, real-world application.

Rather than following a single-state model rigidly, this guide uses a hybrid approach:

- Massachusetts contributes depth, structure, and clarity in early academic foundations
- New Jersey contributes breadth, flexibility, and everyday application through play and conversation

Together, these frameworks support a more complete and usable Pre-K learning experience for young learners.

## Big Picture Comparison

### Math

**Massachusetts**

- Focuses on strong foundations
- Emphasizes counting to 10
- Introduces shapes and spatial awareness
- Builds simple comparison skills

**New Jersey**

- Takes a broader, more applied approach
- Extends counting up to 20
- Introduces early addition and subtraction
- Includes measurement, time, and real-world problem solving

**Instructional Translation**

- Massachusetts offers depth and clarity
- New Jersey offers breadth and real-world use

### Language and Reading

**Massachusetts**

- Uses a slightly more structured language model
- Encourages speaking in complete sentences
- Introduces early grammar awareness

**New Jersey**

- Leans more heavily on interaction-based learning
- Builds language through conversation, play, and social use

**Instructional Translation**

- Massachusetts emphasizes formality and sentence structure
- New Jersey emphasizes function and social communication

## Master Pre-K Curriculum

This hybrid curriculum is designed to capture the best of both models while remaining realistic for a young child. The emphasis should stay on short lessons, repetition, conversation, movement, and play-based reinforcement.

## 1. Math

Recommended lesson length: `15-20 minutes`

### Core Skills from Massachusetts

- Count from `0-10` confidently
- Recognize numerals `0-10`
- Identify basic shapes such as circle, square, and triangle
- Understand comparison language such as `more`, `less`, `one more`, and `one less`

### Extended Skills from New Jersey

- Gradually count up to `20`
- Introduce early addition through simple objects and visuals
- Introduce early subtraction through simple everyday examples
- Explore measurement ideas such as `big`, `small`, `heavy`, and `light`
- Build early time awareness with terms such as `morning`, `afternoon`, and `bedtime`

### Teaching Approaches

- Count snacks during meals and snack time
- Build and sort with blocks
- Compare toys using size, weight, or quantity
- Reuse math language constantly in daily routines

## 2. Language and Communication

### Core Skills from Massachusetts

- Speak in simple, full sentences
- Practice more complete responses such as `"I want juice"` instead of `"juice"`
- Ask basic questions such as `"Where is it?"` and `"What is that?"`

### Extended Skills from New Jersey

- Practice back-and-forth conversation
- Describe objects, actions, and ideas during play
- Name familiar objects in the environment

### Teaching Approaches

- Narrate daily actions and play
- Use descriptive language often, such as `"You are putting the red block on top"`
- Ask open-ended questions during activities
- Offer meaningful choices, such as `"Do you want apple or banana?"`

## 3. Reading Foundations

Both states are relatively light in formal reading expectations at the Pre-K level, which creates a strong opportunity to build a rich literacy foundation without pushing beyond what is developmentally appropriate.

### Combined Approach

- Introduce letter recognition with broad `A-Z` exposure
- Keep phonics light and playful
- Build listening comprehension through read-alouds
- Practice simple story recall

### Teaching Approaches

- Read aloud every day
- Ask simple comprehension questions such as `"What happened?"` and `"Where is the dog?"`
- Point to words while reading
- Use songs and repetition, including alphabet songs

## Recommended Teaching Style

This curriculum works best when instruction is:

- short and consistent
- hands-on and playful
- language-rich
- repetitive without becoming rigid
- connected to real daily experiences

At this age, the goal is not mastery through pressure. The goal is exposure, confidence, repetition, and joyful engagement with learning.

## Practical Curriculum Philosophy

This Pre-K model can be summarized simply:

- use Massachusetts for strong academic foundations
- use New Jersey for applied learning and natural interaction
- teach through routines, play, reading, and conversation

This creates a balanced curriculum that is both structured and flexible, giving young learners a solid start while keeping learning age-appropriate and engaging.
